## 2. What Article Publishers Can Control

### A. Title Optimization
- **Character count**: Target 28-40 characters (30 is ideal)
  - Too long = truncated in Google SERP
  - Too short = missed keyword opportunities
- **Keyword placement**: Put primary keyword at the beginning of the title
- The title becomes both the H1 tag and the `<title>` tag
- Title is the **single most important SEO element** you control on note

### B. Hashtag Strategy
- You can set **up to 10 hashtags** per article (recommended: 3-5 well-chosen ones)
- Hashtags function as **internal links** within note's ecosystem
- Hashtag pages themselves rank on Google (e.g., `note.com/hashtag/ボートレース予想`)
  - If your article is on a high-ranking hashtag page, it gets indirect Google traffic
- **Selection strategy**:
  - Mix popular tags (for discovery) with specific tags (for targeting)
  - Use tags readers would actually search for
  - Avoid spammy over-tagging (4-5 focused tags > 10 random ones)
- Hashtags affect note's **internal discovery algorithm**, not just SEO

### C. Content Structure
- **Headings**: note supports 大見出し (H2) and 小見出し (H3) only
  - Use H2 for main sections, H3 for subsections
  - Include target keywords naturally in H2 headings
  - Maintain proper hierarchy (H2 > H3, never skip levels)
- **Article length**: Longer, comprehensive articles (2000-5000+ characters) tend to rank better
- **First 1-2 sentences are critical**: They become the auto-generated meta description
- **Images**: Always set ALT text on images (contributes to SEO)
- **Readability**: Use line breaks, short paragraphs, bullet points for scannability
- Structure articles so headings alone tell the story (table-of-contents readable)

### D. Internal Linking
- Link to your other note articles within content (increases reader engagement and page views)
- Use **Magazine (マガジン)** feature to group related articles by theme
  - Google recognizes magazines as "clusters of related content"
  - Improves topical authority signals
- Embed URLs of related note articles (they display as rich cards)
- Internal links improve note's perception of your content quality

### E. Profile Optimization
- Profile page is indexed by Google - include target keywords in your bio
- Set a clear, professional profile image
- Write a detailed self-introduction emphasizing expertise (E-E-A-T signals)
- Use a **pinned note** (固定記事) as a landing page for new visitors
- Profile name: consider including your niche keyword if natural

## 3. Custom Meta Tags, OGP, and Structured Data

### What note.com Handles Automatically
- **OGP tags**: Auto-generated from title + cover image + first sentences
- **Twitter Card tags**: Auto-generated
- **Structured data**: note adds basic schema markup automatically
- **Canonical URLs**: Managed by note
- **Sitemap**: Managed by note
- **robots.txt**: Managed by note

### What You CANNOT Customize
- **No custom meta description**: Auto-generated from the first ~120 characters of your article
  - **Workaround**: Write your first 1-2 sentences as if they were a meta description (keyword-rich, compelling summary)
- **No custom HTML in `<head>`**: Cannot add custom meta tags, schema, or tracking scripts
- **No custom CSS/JavaScript**: note strips all custom HTML
- **No anchor links**: Internal page navigation links are not supported
- **No custom OGP images per section**: One cover image = one OGP image
- **No structured data control**: Cannot add FAQ schema, HowTo schema, etc.
- **No canonical URL override**: Cannot point to external pages

### note pro (Paid Business Plan) Adds
- **Custom domain** (e.g., blog.yourdomain.com instead of note.com/username)
  - NOTE: You lose note.com's domain authority if using custom domain
- **Google Analytics integration** (paid option, requires custom domain)
- **Google Search Console** access (with custom domain)
- **Custom navigation menus**
- **Custom color themes**
- Still NO custom meta tags or structured data control

## 4. How note.com Articles Rank on Google Japan

### Ranking Mechanism
- note.com benefits from Google's E-E-A-T evaluation (especially Authority and Trust)
- Articles inherit the domain's backlink profile (millions of backlinks to note.com)
- Google treats note.com as a high-authority UGC platform
- Long-tail keywords are especially viable - less competition within note itself

### Typical Ranking Performance
- Well-optimized note articles can rank on **page 1** for medium-competition keywords
- For high-competition keywords, note articles compete with dedicated websites
- note performs especially well for:
  - "How to" queries
  - Personal experience/review content
  - Niche topics with lower competition
  - Informational intent queries

### Limitations
- You compete with other note users for the same keywords
- Google may show only 1-2 results from note.com per SERP (domain diversity)
- Cannot optimize page speed, Core Web Vitals, or technical SEO factors
- No ability to create topic clusters with URL structure optimization

## 5. Internal Search/Discovery Ranking Factors

### note's Algorithm (Estimated Formula)
**Relevance x Engagement x Account Trust**

### Key Factors:
1. **Title keyword match** - Most heavily weighted for internal search
2. **Initial engagement (初動)** - First 24 hours are critical
   - Articles getting **10+ "suki" (likes) within 24 hours** get algorithmic boost
   - Early comments and shares amplify reach
3. **Account trust score** based on:
   - Posting frequency and consistency
   - Historical article performance
   - Account age
   - Follower count and engagement rate
4. **Content freshness** - Recent articles get temporary boost
5. **Hashtag relevance** - Matching hashtags connect articles to topic feeds
6. **Read completion rate** - Articles read to the end rank higher
7. **Tabs on note**:
   - "人気" (Popular): Overall engagement ranking
   - "急上昇" (Trending): Short-term spike detection
   - "新着" (New): Chronological, all articles appear here briefly
